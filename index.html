Research & Projects


Optimization of Wireless Communication via Coded Cache
Supervised by Prof. Meixiao Tao, in IWCT, SJTU                                                                                    Sep. 2015 - present
        Based on the circumstance that the base stations distribute sparsely while users who will get in and out the network frequently distribute densely, we did analysis of the channel during the process of tansmission and applied coded cache to the whole network aiming at reducing the deliver delay with as low error probability as possible.
        The main technique we used and studied during the research are DOF analysis, interference alignment, interference elimination, symbol extension, index codin and decentralized coded cache.
· Formulated a model combined coded cache with interference channel aiming at minimize the delay in   transmission.
· Applied index coding with side information to K-user interference channel to reduce the delivery load.
· Now deriving the storage-latency tradeoff by decentralized caching under partially connected   interference channel.


Evaluation of Digital Image and Character Recognition
Supervised by Prof. Bin Sheng, in Lab for Digital Media and Data Reconstruction, SJTU                               Sep. 2014 - Aug. 2015
        First we did assesment of digital images in different aspects such as the intensity, the acutance, the grey level and so on. Then a library of sequential images with different levels of quality was established to serve for video deblurring. At last, based on the case in which some old people have difficulty in recognize the information from unpaid bills, we did research in text recognization to extract information from the preprocessed digital images.
        The main technique we used and studied during the research are machine learning, Fourier transform and histogram equalizer.
· Provided reference data of images for the work on deblurring of video sequence.
· Evaluated the digital images using C++ and MATLAB combining BIQI, BRISQUE and NIQE algorithms.
· Enhanced the quality of the blurred images of unpaid bills and did character recognition to facilitate   the bill service.


Remote Information Processing System
Completed at Electronic Engineering Lab., SJTU                                                                                    Mar. 2016 - Jul. 2016
        In this project, we built a system consists of one local master machine, one remote master machine and three slave machine.
        For the master machines, the main function is to both transmit command to the slaves and receive response from them via either WIFI or telephoneline. We designed the communication protocol and built the GUI by our own.
        For the slave machines, the main function are temperature controlling, electric motor driving and distance measurement via ultrasonic, respectively. We designed and welded both the functional circuits and transfer circuits between master and slave.
